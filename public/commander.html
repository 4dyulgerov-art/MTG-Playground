Pod up with friends. Brew weird commanders. Test your new precon upgrade. Free, in your browser.

Commander, fully supported

TCG Playsim treats Commander as a first-class format: command-zone tracking, partner commanders, commander damage, mulligan-to-7 with Commander rules. The deckbuilder validates 100-card singleton lists and lets you tag a card as your commander. At game-start the commander goes to the command zone where you can cast it (paying the commander tax) just like at the table.

Multiplayer rooms support 2 to 4 players in free-for-all. Each player has their own life total, command zone, and shared battlefield.

Building a Commander deck

Click New Deck, set format to Commander, then build with the Scryfall-powered search. The builder shows running totals (mana curve, color identity, type counts) and warns about colour-identity violations. Set any creature or planeswalker that can be a commander as your commander; it's separated into the command zone at game-start.

Playing 4-player Commander

Click Multiplayer → Host, set max players to 4, share the link with three friends. The play interface shows all four players' battlefields with appropriate scaling. Targeting an opponent's permanent is a click; commander damage is tracked per source-player pair so 21-damage threats register correctly.

Why use TCG Playsim for Commander?

Three reasons:
(1) It's free — no card collection grind, every commander and every card is available for brewing.
(2) It's fast — playtesting a Commander deck against a podmate takes a few minutes to set up, not the half-hour of finding tokens, dice, and a flat surface.
(3) It's flexible — Cube draft, custom commanders, homebrew variants all work because the playtester is manual rather than automated.
